Location: Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto City

Higashiyama District

5.0 (500)

Higashiyama District is a beautifully preserved historic area in Kyoto, Japan, known for its traditional architecture and cultural heritage. Nestled along the eastern mountains, this district offers a glimpse into old Kyoto with its narrow lanes, wooden buildings, and charming shops and cafes. Key attractions include Kiyomizu-dera Temple, Yasaka Shrine, and the picturesque streets of Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka, where visitors can explore local crafts, pottery, and traditional sweets. The district is particularly popular during cherry blossom season and autumn foliage, drawing tourists seeking to experience the rich history and ambiance of Kyoto. ​Higashiyama is a vibrant blend of culture, history, and scenic beauty, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.

Things to Do

Tips

  1. Highlights: Traditional wooden buildings, historic streets, local crafts, and major temples.
  2. Duration: Year-round, especially popular during cherry blossom and autumn foliage seasons.
  3. Special festivals and market events throughout the year, particularly during Hanatoro in March.
  • Stroll Through Historic Streets: Experience the charming atmosphere of narrow lanes filled with traditional architecture.
  • Visit Key Temples: Explore iconic sites like Kiyomizu-dera and Ginkaku-ji, renowned for their beauty and cultural significance.
  • Shop for Local Crafts: Discover unique souvenir shops offering pottery, sweets, and traditional crafts.
  • Enjoy Local Cuisine: Try regional specialties at various tea houses and restaurants along the way.
  • Attend Seasonal Festivals: Participate in local events to experience the district's vibrant traditions.
  • Arrive Early: Visit in the morning to avoid crowds, especially at popular attractions.
  • Wear Comfortable Shoes: Be prepared for walking on cobblestone paths and steep inclines.
  • Bring Cash: Many smaller shops and eateries may only accept cash.
  • Check for Festival Dates: Plan visits around annual events for a richer experience.
  • Respect Local Etiquette: Maintain a quiet demeanor, particularly in temples and residences.
  • Capture the Scenery: Bring a camera to photograph the picturesque streets and historic sites.
