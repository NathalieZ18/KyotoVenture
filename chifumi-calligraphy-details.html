Location: Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto City

Chifumi Calligraphy Kyoto

4.7 (300)

Chifumi Calligraphy Kyoto offers an authentic and enriching calligraphy experience in the heart of Kyoto. With expert instructors, guests can dive deep into the traditional art of Japanese calligraphy (shodō), learning how to write kanji, hiragana, or even their own names with graceful brushstrokes. The studio blends modern teaching methods with historical techniques for a memorable cultural experience.

Details

Interest/Topic: Cultural Activities

Popularity: Very High

Reservation: Highly Recommended

Estimated Price Range: Workshop Fee: ¥3,000–¥5,500 ($21–$38 USD)

Optional Upgrades: Framing for your calligraphy: ¥1,000–¥2,500 ($7–$17 USD) Custom brushes or ink sets: ¥500–¥1,000 ($3–$7 USD)

Extra Info

Things to Do

Tips

  1. Cultural Significance: Shodō, or Japanese calligraphy, is a centuries-old practice deeply intertwined with Japanese culture. It is not only about writing, but also about cultivating mindfulness and harmony with the brush. Chifumi’s workshop emphasizes these values while focusing on the meditative process of creating beautiful characters.
  2. Class Structure: Introduction to traditional calligraphy tools, including brushes, ink stones, and paper.
    3. Hands-on instruction: Learn brushstroke techniques and the fundamentals of kanji or hiragana.
  3. Final project: Create your own custom calligraphy artwork to take home.
  • Yasaka Shrine: Just a short walk away, this vibrant shrine is a must-visit for anyone in the Gion district.
  • Explore the Historic Gion District: Wander through this traditional neighborhood, known for its wooden buildings, teahouses, and geisha culture.
  • Visit Kiyomizu-dera: A UNESCO World Heritage Site offering stunning views of the city and ancient Buddhist architecture.
  • Savor Kyoto Cuisine: Try the local delicacies at nearby restaurants, such as yudofu (tofu hot pot) or matcha-flavored desserts.
  • Timing: Book early, as this workshop is a popular choice for tourists. Consider booking a morning session for a more relaxed atmosphere.
  • Dress Comfortably: Wear something comfortable and easy to move in, as you’ll be using your arms to create brushstrokes.
  • Bring Your Ideas: If you have a specific kanji, phrase, or word you’d like to learn, bring it with you! The instructors can help translate and guide you in creating it.
  • Souvenirs: Consider purchasing a custom calligraphy set or framing your finished artwork as a memorable keepsake.
