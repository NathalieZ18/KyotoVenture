Location: Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto City

Gion District

5.0 (500)

​Gion is Kyoto's most renowned geisha district, situated between Yasaka Shrine and the Kamo River.​ The area is characterized by its traditional wooden machiya houses, teahouses (ochaya), and vibrant entertainment scene, where geiko (geisha) and maiko (apprentices) perform to delight guests. Gion not only attracts visitors with its cultural richness and historic charm but also offers a glimpse into the artistry and elegance of Japanese traditions

Things to Do

Tips

  1. Highlights: Geisha culture, historic architecture, traditional teahouses, annual festivals.
  2. Duration: Year-round, with Gion Matsuri in July.
  3. Annual festival Gion Matsuri (Gion Festival) every July.
  • Explore Hanami-koji Street: Stroll through the iconic street lined with teahouses.
  • Visit Yasaka Shrine: Discover the shrine's beauty and significance.
  • Enjoy Evening Walks: Observe geiko and maiko on their way to engagements.
  • Shop for Souvenirs: Browse local crafts and traditional goods.
  • Experience Cultural Performances: Attend events at Gion Corner for traditional arts.
  • Visit Early: Arrive in the morning to enjoy a quieter experience.
  • Drink water to cope with the heat.
  • Stay Mindful: Be respectful of the locals and their traditions.
  • Check Event Schedules: Look for festivals or performances happening during your visit.
  • Use Backstreets: Explore less crowded areas for a more local experience.
  • Bring Cash: Some small shops and teahouses may not accept cards.
